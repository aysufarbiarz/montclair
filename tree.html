Understanding Question 2

The Decision Tree: How your vote impacts the budget

The Starting Problem: A $5.0M Funding Gap for 2025-26.

If you vote NO

Year 1 (2025-26)

Gap filled by Advance State Aid ($5.0M).
(This is a loan that must be repaid)

Required Mid-year Cuts: $2.8M
↓
Year 2 (2026-27)

Loan comes due, plus Less State Aid ($1.85M).

New Funding Gap: $10.0M

Annualized Mid-year Cuts: $3.5M

If you vote YES

Year 1 (2025-26)

Gap filled permanently by Tax Levy ($5.0M).
(No state loan required)

Required Mid-year Cuts: $2.8M
↓
Year 2 (2026-27)

No loan to repay. Base revenue remains higher.

New Funding Gap: $3.7M

Annualized Mid-year Cuts: $3.5M
