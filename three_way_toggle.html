Understand How the Election Outcome Will Impact the Montclair School Deficit

The visuals below are designed to help voters understand how the results of the two special election questions will impact Montclair Public School's (MPS) revenue and expenditures. MPS currently has a budget gap/deficit. Under any vote outcome, MPS will close the gap between revenue and expenditures. Key Terms & Context

Gap: The difference between projected expenditures and projected revenue.
Advance of State Aid (only in a No vote scenario): If MPS gets an advance of future State aid, the State lowers future year State aid amounts to account for the aid it has already advanced (i.e. MPS gets less State aid each year).
Q2 Tax Levy: A Yes vote for Q2 will result in a permanent increase in local tax revenue.
Mid-year Cuts: MPS made cuts to school spending in December 2025 in the amount of $2.8M in 25-26. Those cuts translate to $3.5M of savings for the 26-27 school year because they will be in place all year.
State Monitor: In the event of a No vote for either question, MPS will take an advance of State aid. If the District takes any advance, then the State has indicated it will assign a State Monitor. The District is responsible for the salary of the State Monitor (plus staff). The District has estimated this cost to be $150k per year, although it could be higher.
Interest on Tax Anticipation Notes: This refers to the interest on Tax Anticipation Notes. The District must pay its debts before the tax revenue is collected. These notes allow the District to borrow money as a bridge until the revenue comes in. The District has stated that it must pay interest on this short term bridge.
Cuts Needed: The amount of cuts the District will have to make to balance expenditures and revenues in 26-27 under each voting outcome scenario.
